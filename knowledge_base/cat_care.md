# Cat Care Guidelines

## Feeding Schedule
Adult cats (1–7 years) should be fed twice daily — once in the morning and once in the evening. Portion sizes depend on body weight: typically 20–30 calories per pound of ideal body weight per day. Kittens under 6 months need 3–4 small meals per day to support rapid growth. Senior cats (7+) often do better with smaller, more frequent meals as their digestion slows.

## Indoor Enrichment
Indoor cats require daily mental and physical stimulation to prevent boredom and stress-related behaviors such as over-grooming, aggression, or excessive vocalization.

### Play Sessions
- Provide at least two 10–15 minute interactive play sessions per day using wand toys, laser pointers, or toy mice.
- Rotate toys weekly so cats don't lose interest.
- Play before meals to simulate natural hunt-eat-groom-sleep cycles.

### Environmental Enrichment
- Cat trees and elevated perches allow cats to observe their environment from a safe height.
- Window perches with a bird feeder view provide hours of passive entertainment.
- Paper bags, cardboard boxes, and tunnels offer low-cost exploration opportunities.
- Puzzle feeders and food-dispensing toys encourage foraging behavior.

### Social Interaction
Most cats enjoy 15–30 minutes of gentle interaction daily — petting, brushing, or training with treats. Cats are more social than often assumed; ignoring them entirely leads to stress.

## Litter Box Maintenance
- Scoop litter boxes at least once daily; deep clean weekly.
- Provide one litter box per cat plus one extra (e.g., 2 cats = 3 boxes).
- Place boxes in quiet, accessible locations away from food and water.
- If a cat suddenly stops using the litter box, consult a vet to rule out medical causes.

## Grooming
- Short-haired cats groom themselves but benefit from weekly brushing to reduce hairballs.
- Long-haired cats need daily brushing to prevent matting.
- Trim nails every 2–3 weeks or provide scratch posts.
- Check ears monthly for wax buildup or redness.

## Signs of Stress or Illness
- Hiding more than usual
- Changes in litter box habits
- Decreased appetite or excessive thirst
- Over-grooming or hair loss
- Aggression toward people or other pets
