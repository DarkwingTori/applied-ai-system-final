# Pet Grooming Guidelines

## Dogs

### Brushing by Coat Type

**Short coats** (Boxers, Beagles, Dalmatians):
- Brush once a week with a rubber curry brush or soft-bristle brush to remove loose hair and distribute oils.
- Bathing every 4–6 weeks or as needed.

**Medium coats** (Golden Retrievers, Border Collies, Australian Shepherds):
- Brush 2–3 times per week with a slicker brush and metal comb to prevent tangles.
- Focus on behind the ears, armpits, and groin where mats form first.
- Bathing every 4 weeks.

**Long coats** (Shih Tzus, Maltese, Afghan Hounds):
- Daily brushing is essential to prevent painful matting.
- Professional grooming every 6–8 weeks is typically needed.

**Double coats** (Huskies, Pomeranians, German Shepherds):
- Brush 3–4 times per week; daily during shedding season (spring and fall).
- Use an undercoat rake during heavy shedding to remove the dense undercoat.
- Never shave a double-coated breed — their coat insulates against heat and cold.

### Bathing
- Use dog-specific shampoo; human shampoo disrupts the skin's pH balance.
- Rinse thoroughly to prevent skin irritation from shampoo residue.
- Dry completely after bathing, especially in cold weather or in dogs with skin folds.

### Nail Trimming
- Trim nails every 3–4 weeks or when you hear clicking on hard floors.
- Use sharp dog nail clippers; avoid cutting the quick (pink blood vessel inside the nail).
- If you accidentally cut the quick, apply styptic powder to stop bleeding.
- If nails are very long, trim small amounts weekly rather than cutting back all at once.

### Ear Cleaning
- Check ears weekly for redness, odor, or discharge.
- Clean with a vet-recommended ear cleaner on a cotton ball — never insert anything into the ear canal.
- Floppy-eared breeds (Basset Hounds, Cocker Spaniels) are more prone to infections and may need weekly cleaning.

### Dental Care
- Brush teeth daily using a pet-safe toothpaste (poultry flavor is often accepted).
- Dental chews and water additives help but do not replace brushing.
- Professional dental cleanings are typically needed every 1–3 years depending on the dog.

## Cats

### Brushing
- Short-haired cats: brush once a week.
- Long-haired cats (Persians, Maine Coons): brush daily to prevent matting.
- Use a fine-toothed metal comb to detect mats early; work tangles out gently with your fingers before using a comb.

### Bathing
Most cats do not need regular baths unless they have a skin condition, are very dirty, or are hairless breeds. When bathing is necessary, use cat-specific shampoo and work quickly to minimize stress.

### Nail Trimming
- Trim cat nails every 2–3 weeks.
- Provide scratch posts and pads to allow cats to naturally maintain their nails between trimmings.
- Declawing is considered inhumane by most veterinary organizations and is illegal in many countries.

### Dental Care
- Dental disease affects over 70% of cats by age 3.
- Introduce toothbrushing gradually using tuna-flavored cat toothpaste.
- Dental treats and prescription dental diets can supplement (but not replace) brushing.
