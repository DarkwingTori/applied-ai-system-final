# Dog Exercise Guidelines

## General Walk Recommendations by Breed Size

### Small Breeds (under 20 lbs)
Small dogs such as Chihuahuas, Pomeranians, and Shih Tzus generally need 20–30 minutes of exercise per day, split across 2 short walks. Despite their size, small breeds still require daily movement to prevent weight gain and behavioral issues. Avoid overexertion, especially in heat, as small dogs can overheat quickly.

### Medium Breeds (20–50 lbs)
Medium breeds such as Beagles, Cocker Spaniels, and French Bulldogs thrive with 30–60 minutes of exercise daily. Two 20–30 minute walks per day is a solid routine. These dogs enjoy sniff-heavy walks, which provide mental stimulation in addition to physical exercise.

### Large Breeds (50–90 lbs)
Large breeds such as Labrador Retrievers, Golden Retrievers, and German Shepherds need 60–90 minutes of vigorous exercise daily. At least two 30–45 minute walks, combined with off-leash playtime if possible, helps prevent boredom and destructive behavior.

### Giant Breeds (over 90 lbs)
Giant breeds such as Great Danes, Saint Bernards, and Mastiffs need surprisingly moderate exercise — about 30–45 minutes per day. Joint health is a priority; avoid high-impact activities in puppies under 18 months as their growth plates are still developing.

## High-Energy Breeds
Working, sporting, and herding breeds (Border Collies, Huskies, Jack Russell Terriers) may need 90+ minutes of daily exercise. These dogs benefit from mental enrichment like puzzle feeders, training sessions, and fetch in addition to walks.

## Puppies
Puppies should follow the 5-minute rule: 5 minutes of exercise per month of age, up to twice daily. For example, a 3-month-old puppy needs about 15 minutes per session. Over-exercising puppies can damage developing joints.

## Senior Dogs
Senior dogs (age 7+ for large breeds, 10+ for small breeds) still need daily walks but at a gentler pace. 20–30 minutes twice a day is often appropriate. Watch for signs of fatigue: lagging behind, excessive panting, or limping.

## Weather Considerations
- Hot weather (above 85°F): Walk in early morning or evening; check pavement temperature with your hand.
- Cold weather (below 32°F): Limit walks for short-haired or small breeds; consider dog boots and a coat.
- Always provide fresh water after walks.

## Signs Your Dog Needs More Exercise
- Destructive chewing or digging
- Excessive barking or whining
- Hyperactivity indoors
- Weight gain
- Pulling hard on the leash (pent-up energy)
